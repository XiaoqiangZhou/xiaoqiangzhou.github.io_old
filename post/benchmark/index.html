How to Make a Benchmark Analysis

List some papers that propose a benchmark in their areas.

Xiaoqiang Zhou
Last updated on Dec 16, 2020 4 min read Paper notes

[CVPR 2019] Single Image Deraining: A Comprehensive Benchmark Analysis

Motivation:

It is thus unclear how these algorithms would perform on rainy images acquired “in the wild” and how we could gauge the progress in the field.

Pipeline:
  1. Formulate the rainy image synthesis process (from simple to complex)
  2. Summarize the contribution (thorough methods/testing sets)
  3. Method: Introduce the training sets
  4. Method: Introduce the testing sets
  5. Method: Introduce the additional task-driven (object detection in rainy images) testing sets.
  6. Experiments: Objective comparison (PSNR, SSIM, NIQE, SSEQ, and BLIINDSII)
  7. Experiments: Subjective comparison (11 human raters)

[TPAMI 2021] Cross-Domain Facial Expression Recognition: A Unified Evaluation Benchmark and Adversarial Graph Learning

Motivation:
  1. Comprehensive and fair comparisons are lacking due to inconsistent choices of the source/target datasets and feature extractors.
  2. This journal paper is an extension work on their conference paper, which is published on ACM MM 2020. Therefore, the authors propose a novel method for this task.
Pipeline:
  1. Category existing works by the training sets and backbone network.
  2. Build a simple baseline with ResNet-50 and take experiments to verify the influence of domain gap caused by the selected training sets.
  3. Conduct experiments to compare the effectiveness of different backbones under a baseline design.
  4. Re-implement some SOTA methods manually and take thorough experiments with different training sets / backbone.
  5. Evaluation Protocols: Dataset choice/Feature extractor choice
  6. Introduce the proposed method.
  7. Ablation study on the proposed method.
